Role Terms





GTA V Roleplay (FiveM) is a game. We may sometimes

engage in unrealistic movements and behaviors.




There are certain rules and roleterms that help make

roleplay as realistic as possible.



In Character (IC)

This means "in-character." It shouldn't have any connection to the real world. For example, if you're friends with someone in real life and haven't met them while playing a role,

you shouldn't call them by name when you run into each other on the street. This also causes a Metagame bug.





Out Of Character (OOC)

This includes events that are out of character. For example, someone dropped out mid-role or deliberately left the game. It's used to write things that aren't part of the role,

such as "/ooc Where did he go?"





Random DeathMatch (RDM)

Killing someone for no reason, without a role, or without someone having enough of a role to play.

Examples of roles involving RDM

- Someone crashes into your car. If you kill them just because they tell you to "Drive slow!", that's RDM.
